Managing money is important for everyone and most people don’t know how to manage money as they lack basic knowledge on how to do that. It is also a sensitive topic especially when most of our understanding about money and financial habits are inherited from our parents and the people around us.

As a person who had been using an excel sheet for managing money for a very long time, I know the importance of the financial discipline. At the same time, I know how hard it is to generate insights based on the feed data. Even though I had written various VBA coding on top of Excel functions to automate the financial insights, I always had to tweak here and there to get it working.

So, one day I decided to build the same in an online web app. That way, I build the application only once, and it would be available to anyone worldwide who are new to using personal financial statements.

There began, the story of a lone developer's quest to build an online financial insight generating platform. Just one problem, I was not a web developer.


I used to do some backend programming for IoT platforms at my workplace. That is about it. I had zero knowledge on the front-end. Moreover, I felt just a tad bit overwhelmed by all the languages, frameworks, and learning resources out there. Nevertheless, it took a couple of months and after tabs and tabs of google search results, I got better at JavaScript, HTML, CSS, Git, Node.JS etc. These are only coding and tools. They are learnable and practicable. The devil lies in the details, they say. In my case, the devil was the UX designing part.

UX Design : User experience (UX) is an art to create interactions that not only look and feel great, but also work extremely well. It refers to the quality of the user's interaction with the website.

It would have taken another year to learn and implement just that. Thought of leaving the UX/UI development to the experts. Buying a theme is a great way to speed up production but I still need to know what I'm are doing. Spend many hours before buying the right theme with the proper documentation.

Still, there was a lot of front-end programming to do on top of that. I started one page a time, and day by day, the structure come to life. There were several occasions, where I had to stop and start from scratch, as I had not planned for provisions prior – beginners mistakes, but each time, I had learnt something new.

Unfortunately, Trail & Error method of learning brings out mostly errors it seems. I learnt a lot. Trail & Error method will ultimately yield success, but not effective. If you let an inexperienced person to build a large ship, he obviously can’t, but if give him a few decades he can. That is how efficient Trail & Error method is.

Although I had understood most of the key concepts of the JavaScript, one concept which gave me more trouble than the rest is "Promises". As the humorous comic above illustrates, dealing with asynchronous programming was hard. Waiting for the “eventual consistency” was confusing and hard to deal with, but "promises" were crucial part of the back-end programming, as the data and the graphical elements did not get synchronized. As a result, the site lagged and was not very responsive.

It took attempts after attempts to conquer the last monster.

One by one the challenges were brought down and in no time, I had a fully functioning website with minor glitches and errors that I had to patch up. In the course, I learned a lot of cyber security and HTTPS concepts to make the site more secure. I finally bought the “Adminiate” domain and hosted the application.

Later, I had included feedback pages, about us, and even this page as well. I think I have cleared most of the issues. I'm continously improving the site based your feedback.

Any thoughts, ideas and suggestions are welcome.
